About Corona Virus

Corona Virus, commonly referred to as COVID-19, is caused by a virus known as SARS-CoV-2. This virus, part of the coronavirus family, emerged in Wuhan, China, in late 2019 and rapidly spread worldwide, causing a global pandemic. COVID-19 primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and close contact with infected individuals, making it highly contagious.

Coronavirus what it is?

Coronaviruses are named for the crown-like spikes on their surface, resembling a "corona" or halo. They are RNA viruses, meaning their genetic material is ribonucleic acid (RNA). This particular structure allows them to spread and mutate easily, which can result in new strains.

How to Protect Yourself

To protect yourself from coronavirus (COVID-19), follow these essential guidelines recommended by health experts:

Wear Mask

Wear a well-fitted mask, especially in crowded or enclosed spaces, to reduce the chance of inhaling or spreading viral particles.

Wash Your Hands

Frequently wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in public places.

Stay at Home

Improve air circulation by opening windows and using air purifiers, which reduces the concentration of viral particles indoors.

Coronavirus Cases

COVID-19 has impacted millions globally, with cases documented in nearly every country. The count of active, recovered, and severe cases is regularly updated to keep people informed.

Read More

Cases 01

Since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, case tracking has been a fundamental part of understanding how the virus spreads. Each case represents a person who has been infected, and analyzing patterns helps health experts predict trends and potential surges. Active cases reflect those currently battling the virus, while recovered cases indicate those who have successfully overcome it.

Read More

Cases 02

With each case of COVID-19, health authorities track details to understand the severity and progression of the illness. Monitoring the number of mild and severe cases helps in assessing the health system's needs. Hospitals and clinics worldwide contribute data to centralized platforms, giving a clearer picture of where resources are most needed.

Read More

Cases 03

COVID-19 cases are documented in real-time to help governments and health organizations respond appropriately. This ongoing monitoring also supports vaccine and treatment research, providing scientists with crucial data. The updates on new cases, recoveries, and fatalities keep the public informed and guide public health decisions globally.

What doctors say..

Dr. Maria Van Kerkhove

Dr. Maria Van Kerkhove emphasizes that one of the most important ways to protect yourself from COVID-19 is to follow preventive measures, such as frequent handwashing, wearing a mask, and getting vaccinated. He explains that COVID-19 spreads primarily through respiratory droplets, so limiting close contact with others and staying in well-ventilated areas can reduce transmission risks.

Dr. Zhong Nanshan

According to Dr. Zhong Nanshan, early testing and isolation are essential if someone shows symptoms of COVID-19, like cough, fever, or difficulty breathing. He also highlights the importance of staying updated with booster vaccinations, especially for high-risk groups, to prevent severe illness.
